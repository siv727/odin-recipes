Stir-Fry Ramen

Description

While I love a good bowl of Tonkotsu Ramen, this recipe is more of a complete dinner!

Packed full of all your favorite stir fry vegetables, super filling thanks to ramen noodles, and tossed in the best stir fry sauce to take the flavor over the top.

One of my favorite parts of stir fry is pairing it with either white rice or noodles. Ramen noodles are particularly delicious in stir fry, and the perfect texture! They are a great all purpose noodle to use in basically any stir fry recipe.

Ingredients

Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup of hoisin sauce
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 (3 ounce) packages ramen noodles (without flavor packet)

Stir-Fry

  • 2 teaspoons peanut oil
  • 1 bunch asparagus, cut diagonally into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced diagonally
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, pressed
  • 1 cup of thinly sliced cooked chicken
  • 2 cups sliced napa cabbage
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms

Steps:

  1. Whisk hoisin sauce, water, cornstarch, sugar, ginger, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper together in a bowl to make sauce.
  2. Bring water to a boil in a large pot. Add ramen noodles. Cook, stirring occasionally, until tender, about 3 minutes. Drain.
  3. Heat peanut oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add asparagus, carrots, onion, and garlic; saute until slightly tender, 3 to 5 minutes.
  5. Add chicken; toss until warmed through, about 2 minutes.
  6. Add cabbage and mushrooms. Pour in sauce; toss until coated.
  7. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until flavors combine, 3 to 5 minutes more.
  8. Serve sauteed asparagus mixture over noodles.
